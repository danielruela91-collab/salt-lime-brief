How does the offer actually get redeemed?

Popmenu handles it with codes, or the restaurant team handles it on the floor.

A
Popmenu offer

Build the on-premise offer in Popmenu — single-use codes per guest

Daniel creates the Mother's Day offer inside Popmenu with a May 10 validity window. The system generates a unique code per guest. That code goes out by email and is scanned or entered at the table to redeem.

What this requires Daniel builds the offer in Popmenu before anything goes live. Code must be valid and attached to the email send before the campaign launches.
Tracked redemption — Popmenu records who used it
Fraud-proof, one code per person
Setup time required before anything can go out
B
No setup needed

Announce it and let the restaurant team handle redemption on premise

No codes, no Popmenu configuration. The deal is communicated and staff honors it at the table when guests show up May 10 with a photo of their kids. Simple, fast to launch.

What this requires Briefing the staff before Mother's Day so they know the deal and how to apply it at the POS. No digital setup needed.
No Popmenu setup — campaign can go live immediately
No friction for the guest
No tracking, no redemption data

What does the social post do?

Just tell people, or use the moment to grow the VIP list.

← Complete Step 1 first
A
Open announcement

Just announce it — tell everyone the deal exists, no CTA beyond "come in"

The post puts the offer out in the world. No sign-up required, no VIP wall. Anyone who sees it can walk in May 10 and redeem. Pure reach play.

Caption direction "Moms get $1 margaritas this Mother's Day 🌿 Show up May 10, show us a photo of your kids. All 3 locations, dine-in only."
Maximum reach, zero barrier
No list growth, no new VIPs captured
B
VIP — this deal only

Gate this specific offer behind VIP signup — the deal is the reason to join

The post makes clear: $1 margaritas are exclusive to VIPs. Sign up on the website, and the offer lands in the welcome email automatically. The Mother's Day deal is the hook.

Caption direction "$1 margaritas for moms May 10 — but only for VIPs. Sign up at saltandlimeaz.com before Sunday to unlock it. Link in bio."
Direct list growth, every sign-up is a lead
People who don't sign up can't participate
C
VIP — all offers

Use Mother's Day as the hook to sell the whole VIP program, not just this deal

The post promotes becoming a VIP in general. Mother's Day is the timely reason to join now. Goal is building a list that keeps paying off campaign after campaign — not just May 10 traffic.

Caption direction "VIPs get exclusive deals all year — and we have something special lined up for Mother's Day. Join the list now and find out. Link in bio."
Long-term list value, not just one campaign
Less specific — lower urgency, lower conversion
