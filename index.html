Four ways to run
the $1 margarita campaign

Each approach has a different level of setup, tracking, and Popmenu involvement. Kaese — pick the one that fits.

The offer, in every case: $1 margaritas for moms on May 10, dine-in only. Show a photo of your kids. All 3 locations. The strategies below differ in how the offer is delivered, tracked, and redeemed.
1
Setup required

Popmenu on-premise offer with single-use codes

Daniel creates the offer inside Popmenu first — as an on-premise offer with a validity window tied to May 10. Popmenu generates a single-use code per guest. The code is attached to the email they receive.

How it flows
Daniel builds offer in Popmenu → System generates unique codes → Code sent via email → Guest redeems on May 10
Fully tracked, fraud-proof
Most setup time upfront
Popmenu handles redemption
Codes must be valid before send
2
Current setup

VIP welcome email — social drives sign-ups

The offer lives in the existing Popmenu welcome automation. Social posts drive new people to sign up as VIPs on the website. The moment they join, the offer lands in their inbox automatically.

How it flows
Social post goes live → Guest signs up as VIP → Welcome email with offer → Redeem by May 10
Grows the list permanently
Needs welcome email configured
Reusable for future campaigns
Requires social post ASAP
3
Low setup

Announcement only — staff handles redemption

Communicate the offer on social and via email blast to the existing list — no codes, no automation. The restaurant team handles how margs are given out on the floor. Pure awareness play.

How it flows
Email + social go out → Guest sees the offer → Walks in May 10 → Staff honors it on premise
Fastest to execute
No tracking or redemption data
Zero Popmenu config needed
Relies entirely on staff
4
Soft approach

Teaser social + "last call" VIP urgency

Social posts hint at a Mother's Day surprise without naming the specific offer. The hook is urgency: "become a VIP before May 10 to unlock a surprise." A last-call post goes out close to the date. Intrigue over specifics.

How it flows
Teaser post — no offer details → CTA: become a VIP now → Last-call post pre–May 10 → VIPs get the reveal
Builds curiosity + urgency
Offer less clear upfront
Drives list growth via FOMO
Depends on follow-through timing
